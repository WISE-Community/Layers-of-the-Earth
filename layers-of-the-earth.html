The crust is the outermost layer of the Earth.

It is a solid layer that includes both dry land (continental crust) and the ocean floor (oceanic crust).

The mantle layer begins about 25 miles below the surface.

It is a mostly solid layer of very hot rock. Parts of it can flow very slowly like a fluid because of intense heat and pressure. It moves much slower than your fingernails grow. It takes about 100 million years for rock in the mantle to move between the crust and the outer core.

The outer core is a liquid layer of melted metal, mainly iron and nickle.

The inner core is a solid layer of iron and nickle.

The lithosphere is a combination of the crust and the uppermost layer of the mantle.

It is very rigid nd does not flow. It contains the tectonic plates.

The asthenosphere is the layer of the mantle beneath the lithosphere.

It is mostly solid rock. Parts of it can flow very slowly like a fluid because of intense heat and pressure. It moves much slower than your fingernails grow. It takes about 100 million years for rock in the mantle to move between the crust and the outer core.
